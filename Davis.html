Aloki Case Study

Davis Computer Science Club Webpage

As president of the Davis Computer Science Club I sought to renovate our club’s page to be a concise one pager that is consistent and brief in our organization’s goal and mission.

Role
UI/UX Intern
Time Frame
9 weeks
Opportunity!

I learned what it was like to coordinate two different teams in conjunction with one another to lead them to a concise final product.

Process

Research

What is wrong with the current site?

The current website has a lot of room for improvement. With a very elementary use of color and layout the web-page is very basic and just barely gets the job done. I want a web-page for this organization that conveys moods that can be defined as sleek and clean. In general the current website is very cluttered and is hard on the eyes in terms of color. It’s many pages are also lacking in sense of layout and general structure. Instead of a overly lengthy and messy web page I wanted to make a concise one pager that is consistent and brief in our organization’s goal and mission.

Target Audience

When approaching this design challenge I knew it was important to narrow down who the portfolio website was for. After developing some target audience cards I understood that I could break down the points of interaction into four categories: New businesses, new clients, potential employees, and a fourth miscellaneous contact for those who do not fit into the 3 preconceived audience members.

Ideation

Mood Boarding

Before designing the web-page I wanted to look at potential inspiration that could guide the design process.
Highlights:

  • The use of motion in the hero image to convey “technologically advanced” mood.
  • Sleek use of color to convey contemporary themes.
  • Concise web-page that is easily navigable and establishes purpose naturally with ease.

Design System

Before composing the one-pager with my team I wanted to make a Design Guide to ensure all the sections of the page looked consistent. I purposefully chose the fonts and colors to guide a composition that could be described as modern, sleek, and tech oriented. This part of the ideation was critical as when working with a team of different designers you do not want any discrepancies between the different sections of the page.

Composing

Landing Page Layout and Corresponding Forms

Insight

Some Additional Things I Kept In Mind

  • Page Hierarchy - knowing the sequential order in which I wanted to display the different categories of contact was important to making a page with Aloki’s priorities as a company in mind
  • Error Checking - What if someone tries to submit an empty form? I made sure to put error checking in case of this mistake.
  • Thank you/Confirmation - Speaking from personal experience I hate submitting a form and not getting any confirmation or indication I made an impact. I ensured to thank the user for filling out the form in addition to giving them an estimate of when they would hear back.

Developing

Coding the Prototype

Phase One:
To start off the project I presented the mobile and desktop prototype in Figma to the coding team. After assigning different sections to different members of the tech team I set up a git hub repository to ensure there was a consolidated place for them to upload their progress.

Phase Two:
After receiving each team’s deliverables I ensured to revise it to look for discrepancies between the prototype and the code. After pointing out numerous errors for revision, the team had additional work to do to complete the prototype to completion.

Phase Three:
We were getting close to the end after consolidating the Desktop view. I led a meeting in which we ensured that the page was adequately equipped to handle all views and to ensure there were no blatant errors when doing so. After everything was ironed out it was time to launch the site.

Phase Four:
Finally we chose _____ to host the website and changed the URL over to the new version of the web-page. The project with the help of my great team at DCSC had been completed.
