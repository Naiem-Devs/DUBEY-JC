Inner Eye Arts Case Study

Inner Eye Arts Website Rehaul

In the spring quarter of 2023, I spent 12 weeks with the Inner Eye Arts board where I was tasked to rehaul their entire website to feel more contemporary and for the website to more accurately reflect the brand identity of the organization.

Team
Sach Dubey
Role
UI/UX Intern
Time Frame
12 weeks
Opportunity!

I learned the valuable skill of bringing an organizations’ brand into the modern age via more contemporary design elements.

Process

Research

What is wrong with the current site?

I started the project by analyzing what aspects of the website made it seem outdated. A lot of the problems stemmed from the fact it was made in Weebly with little design expertise. Missing the sleekness of most contemporary websites the page lacked cleanliness and felt clunky in the way texts were dropped into preconceived boxes and templates. After taking the time to pick apart the mistakes of the website I learned what elements of a page may seem outdated and what contemporary solutions to this may look like. 

Competitive Analysis

To learn what modern websites look like in the genre I took some time to perform some competitive analysis to Inner Eye Art’s sister organizations. I took the time to dissect what I loved about these sister organization sites and conceptualized how these aspects could be incorporated into the new Inner Eye Arts Website.

  • consistent branding
  • large hero images
  • sleek navigation
  • beautiful use of motion animation
  • consistent branding
  • large hero images
  • sleek navigation
  • beautiful use of motion animation

Ideation

Affinity Mapping

I took the time to compile what was missing in the current website into an Affinity Map to further gauge what needs to be performed during the design process. After sharing my findings with the Inner Eye Arts board I sought to further ideate the website

Mood Boarding

Before visually designing, I wanted to compose a moodboard dedicated to exploring how a logo can influence web design.

For instance
  • The AAWAA logo’s colors are consistent with the colors used on a page in an effort to acheive consistent branding
  • In a similar manner I want the Inner Eye logo to be utilized in the same way.

Organizing

User Flow

Before starting to compose the final composition I wanted to set up a user flow of all the possibilities of interactions on the site to ensure that any page in the interaction is not missed and that everything is accounted for.

Envisioning

Features To Be Implemented

I am currently working on additional features for the website beyond the ones of the scope of the project. I am attempting to add a shop where Inner Eye Merch could be sold. In addition instead of linking an external page for the organization’s head to update for the archive I wanted to make a map feature that visually lays out where Inner Eye Art’s affiliated shows have been in the past. I am expected to launch these features to the website the summer of 2023.

Composing

Landing Page Layout

  • Page Hierarchy - knowing the sequential order in which I wanted to display the different categories of contact was important to making a page with Aloki’s priorities as a company in mind
  • Error Checking - What if someone tries to submit an empty form? I made sure to put error checking in case of this mistake.
  • Thank you/Confirmation - Speaking from personal experience I hate submitting a form and not getting any confirmation or indication I made an impact. I ensured to thank the user for filling out the form in addition to giving them an estimate of when they would hear back.

Reflection

In Conclusion.....

I learned a lot from this project especially what modern solutions look like when approaching a redesign of an outdated website. I learned about the importance and power of typography and how the feeling of the page is largely because of the small intricacies composing it. I learned the power of spacing, and the importance of consistency when approaching a large size page. I would say I am most proud of my use of color to intentionally spotlight the visually intense images the organization has to offer. I found revising it over three times and comparing my final draft to my first an extremely satisfying and rewarding process. I also feel like I consolidated what works for me in terms of planning an extensive UI/UX project as to not get overwhelmed by the scope of the project. I am looking forward to launching this site at the end of spring 2023 and I am happy to contribute to such an impactful organization.
